About The Cormorant

Four generations, one harbor.

The inn hasn't changed hands outside the family since the day it opened. What's changed is the plumbing.

Our Story

Built for the boats, kept for the guests.

The Cormorant began as lodging for harbor pilots waiting out bad weather — a few bunks above a chandlery, a stove that never went cold. When the pilots' work moved elsewhere in the 1960s, the building stayed in the same family and slowly became something else: a small inn for people who wanted the harbor without the work of running a boat.

We still keep the pilots' logbook by the front desk. Guests are welcome to read it, and to add a line of their own before they go.

Getting Here

Easier to find than to leave.

The Cormorant sits at the end of Quay Street, a five-minute walk from the train halt and an easy drive from the coastal road. Parking is limited to six spaces — let us know if you're driving and we'll hold one.

  • 5 min walk from Pebble Quay Halt
